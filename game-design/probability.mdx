---
title: "Probability Model"
description: "How outcome probabilities work, visibility tiers, and shift mechanics"
---


Each event has three possible outcomes. At the start of an era, they are distributed evenly: two at 33% and one at 34%. From there, players spend their action rounds trying to shift those numbers in their favour.

Three constraints hold throughout:

- The floor is **0%** and the ceiling is **90%** — no outcome can be guaranteed or made impossible
- All three outcomes always sum to **100%**; shifting one redistributes the remainder proportionally across the other two
- Exact probabilities are **hidden by default** — only a player who played `SCAN` targeting that event in the current round can see them

## Banded probability reveal

After Action Round 2 closes, **probability bands** are publicly revealed:

| Band | Range |
|---|---|
| LOW | 0–30% |
| MEDIUM | 31–60% |
| HIGH | 61–90% |

Bands reveal the *results* of Round 2, not in-progress plays. Exact state remains private — Scan card only.

This creates a deliberate two-tier visibility model:

- **Band** — public, post-Round-2, available to all players
- **Exact** — private, Scan card only

Bands communicate directional momentum; exact values remain a strategic advantage for players who use Scan.

## Example shift

Starting state: A=40%, B=35%, C=25%

Player plays `PUSH` targeting outcome A (+20%):
- A goes from 40% → 60% (+20)
- B and C lose proportionally: B → 35 - 20×(35/60) ≈ 23%, C → 25 - 20×(25/60) ≈ 17%
- Result: A=60%, B=23%, C=17% (sums to 100%)

## Scan card

`SCAN` reveals the exact current probability state of one event to the player who played it. The result arrives via the `ProbabilityStateCalculated` event, which the notification module delivers only to the player who submitted the Scan — it is never broadcast.
