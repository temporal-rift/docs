# Probability Model

## Rules

- Each outcome starts at **33% probability** (equal baseline; one starts at 34%)
- Floor: **0%** — ceiling: **90%** (no outcome can be guaranteed)
- All 3 outcomes always sum to **100%**
- Shifting one outcome redistributes proportionally across the other two
- Exact probabilities are **hidden by default** — only visible to a player who played `SCAN` targeting that event

## Banded probability reveal

After Action Round 2 closes, **probability bands** are publicly revealed:

| Band | Range |
|---|---|
| LOW | 0–30% |
| MEDIUM | 31–60% |
| HIGH | 61–90% |

Bands reveal the *results* of Round 2, not in-progress plays. Exact state remains private — Scan card only.

This is a two-tier model:
- **Band** — public, post-Round-2, available to everyone
- **Exact** — private, Scan card only

Any new mechanic that reveals probability information must be evaluated against this two-tier model. Do not conflate them.

## Example shift

Starting state: A=40%, B=35%, C=25%

Player plays `PUSH` targeting outcome A (+20%):
- A goes from 40% → 60% (+20)
- B and C lose proportionally: B → 35 - 20×(35/60) ≈ 23%, C → 25 - 20×(25/60) ≈ 17%
- Result: A=60%, B=23%, C=17% (sums to 100%)

## Scan card

`SCAN` reveals the exact current probability state of one event to the player who played it. This information is delivered via the `ProbabilityStateCalculated` event — filtered so only the Scan player receives it.
